Consultant's Verdict

This website has a good heart but a weak digital presence. The cause is compelling, the testimonials are real, and the team is doing legitimate humanitarian work. But the site is built on a locked, developer-dependent platform that prevents growth — and the donation flow, visual design, and content strategy are all leaving significant engagement on the table. The good news: the gap between where this site is and where it needs to be is entirely closeable with the right rebuild strategy.

Section 01
Current Website Audit
🔴 Critical — Platform Dependency

The site is hosted on The Local Discovery — a proprietary platform. The client has zero ability to update pages, change images, add campaigns, or manage content independently. Every change requires paying a developer. This is a fundamental problem that blocks all marketing and growth.

🔴 Critical — Broken Donation UX
  • Donation "packages" send users to raw Stripe payment links — no branded checkout, no confirmation page, no thank-you email automation.
  • One-time donations go to a generic Stripe link with zero context.
  • There is a QR code image on screen — this is a physical-world workaround embedded in a digital page. Completely ineffective online.
  • No donor receipt email, no recurring dashboard, no donation tracking for donors.
🟡 High — Homepage Issues
  • The hero section leads with a hashtag-heavy block of social media copy — this is pasted from Instagram/Facebook, not written for a website. Feels unprofessional.
  • The mission statement is duplicated twice on the homepage — "Our Mission" and "Your Mission" contain nearly identical copy.
  • Stats (157 volunteers, $45,624 raised) are shown but not contextualized — no year, no "what this achieved," no emotional anchor.
  • Navigation is flat and generic — "Make a Difference" is vague; "Johnny Davis Ministries" creates brand confusion alongside "Johnny Davis Global Missions."
🟡 High — Content & SEO Gaps
  • No blog, no news, no updates — Google has nothing to index beyond 5 thin pages.
  • No meta descriptions, no structured data, no Open Graph tags confirmed.
  • Page title is generic: "Non-Profit Organization near Suwanee, GA" — misses every keyword opportunity related to Philippines missions, child hunger, disaster relief.
  • Images are hosted on a CDN with non-descriptive filenames — no alt text strategy evident.
🔵 Observe — What's Working
  • Three genuine, location-specific testimonials — rare and valuable for a small nonprofit.
  • Real field photos from the Philippines — authentic visual content.
  • Clear cause areas: hunger, disaster relief, clean water, trafficking, education, medical.
  • Stripe integration is already live — just needs a proper wrapper.
  • The $7.99/month entry point is smart — low friction recurring giving.
Section 02
Core Problems Diagnosed
🔒

Critical Zero Client Control

Every content change requires a developer. The client cannot launch a campaign, post an update, or add a donation appeal without paying for it. This alone kills agility and marketing speed.

💸

Critical Donation Funnel Leaks

Raw Stripe links = no storytelling, no social proof at checkout, no thank-you flow, no email capture, no repeat donor cultivation. Every donor who clicks "Donate" exits the brand experience immediately.

📉

High No Email List Growth

There is no newsletter signup anywhere on the site. This means zero ability to nurture donors, share impact updates, or re-engage lapsed supporters. Email is the highest-ROI channel for nonprofits.

🗺️

High No Volunteer Pipeline

"Contact us to learn about volunteering" is not a system. There's no intake form, no upcoming events calendar, no way to track interest or organize volunteer capacity.

📸

High Weak Impact Storytelling

The site lists what it does, but doesn't show outcomes. No story format ("Here's how Maria's family got clean water because of your donation"), no impact metrics page, no gallery of field work.

🔍

High Invisible to Search

With 5 thin pages and no blog, this site cannot rank for any meaningful terms. People searching "Philippines child hunger nonprofit," "disaster relief Philippines donation," or "volunteer Philippines mission" will never find this organization.

Section 03 · Highest Priority
CMS & Admin System Recommendation
✅ Primary Recommendation: WordPress + Elementor Pro

WordPress powers 43% of all websites and is the dominant platform for nonprofits globally. With Elementor Pro as the page builder and GiveWP as the donation plugin, the client gets full control of every page, image, post, campaign, and form — no developer needed for content changes.

Section 04
Marketing & Conversion Strategy
Donation Funnel — Rebuild Completely
  • Replace raw Stripe links with branded donation pages built inside WordPress using GiveWP — each cause gets its own page with a story, photos, goal meter, and impact breakdown.
  • Add suggested amounts with context: "$7.99 feeds 1 child for a month," "$29.99 feeds a family," "$99 sends medicine to a village" — not just dollar amounts.
  • Add a post-donation thank-you page with a personal message from Pastor Johnny, a shareable social card, and a "tell a friend" prompt.
  • Trigger an automated email welcome sequence for every new donor (receipt → mission story → impact update → 30-day follow-up).
  • Add a recurring donor program with a name: "The Compassion Circle" or "Feed a Generation Monthly Partners."
Email Marketing — Start Immediately
  • Add newsletter signup everywhere: homepage hero, footer, after blog posts, on the donation page, at the end of impact stories.
  • Offer a lead magnet: "Download our 2025 Mission Impact Report" or "See the faces your giving has fed" (a photo report PDF).
  • Use Mailchimp (free up to 500 contacts) connected to the WordPress site — fully manageable by the client.
  • Send a monthly "Field Update" email — photos from the Philippines, a story, and a call to action. This alone can double repeat donations.
SEO — Long-Term Organic Growth
  • Rewrite all page title tags and meta descriptions with targeted keywords: "Philippines child hunger relief," "disaster relief Cebu Philippines," "feed Filipino children nonprofit."
  • Create a blog/news section with monthly mission updates — each post targets a searchable topic and builds domain authority over time.
  • Add structured data markup (nonprofit schema) so Google knows this is a registered charity.
  • Set up Google for Nonprofits — free $10,000/month in Google Ads for nonprofits. Currently not in use. This alone could drive hundreds of new donors monthly.
  • Register in nonprofit directories: Charity Navigator, GuideStar/Candid, GiveWell, GlobalGiving.
Storytelling — The Highest-Converting Content Type
  • Create a dedicated "Stories of Impact" section with individual stories: real names (with permission), before/after situations, photos, and outcomes.
  • Add a featured story on the homepage — rotating, with a "Read Maria's Story" CTA that leads directly to a donation ask.
  • Film short 1–2 minute videos in the Philippines — even phone footage with subtitles converts extraordinarily well for mission nonprofits.
  • Feature Pastor Johnny's personal testimony as a prominent "Why I started this" narrative — founders' stories build massive trust.
Section 05
Feature Enhancements to Build
📰

Blog / Mission Updates

Monthly field reports, mission stories, and prayer requests. Builds SEO, keeps supporters engaged, and gives donors something to share on social media.

🙋

Volunteer Intake System

A form where prospective volunteers enter name, location, skills, and availability. Feeds a spreadsheet or CRM. Currently there's just "contact us." This loses hundreds of volunteer signups annually.

📅

Events Calendar

List upcoming outreach events, fundraisers, and mission trips. Allow visitors to RSVP or register — even for virtual prayer events. Creates ongoing reasons to return to the site.

📊

Impact Dashboard

A visual "Impact Counter" on the homepage: meals served, families helped, wells built, children in school. Updated monthly. Nothing converts donors like seeing concrete numbers grow over time.

🖼️

Field Photo Gallery

A well-organized gallery by mission, year, or location. Show the real work happening on the ground. Currently field photos exist but are scattered and unsearchable.

🌏

Multi-Language Support

Add Filipino (Tagalog) as a language option. Many donors, community members, and potential Filipino-American supporters would engage far more with a site in their language. WPML plugin handles this in WordPress.

🏷️

Campaign-Specific Pages

Instead of one generic "Make a Difference" page, create individual campaign pages: "Feed Filipino Children," "Cebu Earthquake Relief," "Uganda Water Wells." Each tells a specific story with a specific giving goal.

🛡️

Trust & Transparency Page

Financial summary, 990 tax form link, board of directors, IRS determination letter, and partner organizations. This is what institutional donors and grant-makers look for before committing funds.

Section 06
UI / UX Improvement Plan
Homepage — Redesign Priorities
  • Hero section: Replace the social media copy paste with a single powerful headline ("Every $7.99 feeds a child for a month"), a human photo or short autoplay video (muted), and one primary CTA button: "Donate Now — Feed a Child." One decision, not five.
  • Remove the QR code from the website — this is only useful in printed materials, not on screens.
  • Impact counter bar: Add a scrolling stat row right below the hero — meals served, volunteers, countries, families helped — visually animated as numbers.
  • Cut duplicate content: "Our Mission" and "Your Mission" are nearly identical. Merge into one and replace the second with a real story or video.
  • Add a persistent "Donate" button in the navigation that stays visible on scroll. Currently there's no top-nav donation trigger.
Navigation — Restructure
  • Current: Home · Who We Are · What We Do · Make a Difference · Johnny Davis Ministries · Contact
  • Recommended: Home · About Us · Our Work ▾ · Missions ▾ · Ways to Give · Volunteer · Blog · Contact · [Donate Button]
  • Separate "Johnny Davis Ministries" vs "Johnny Davis Global Missions" branding confusion — pick one identity or clearly differentiate the ministries arm on a dedicated page.
  • Add a mega-menu or dropdown under "Our Work" with quick links to hunger, disaster relief, clean water, trafficking, education — each linking to a dedicated page.
Design Language — Modernize
  • Adopt a consistent color palette: deep navy + warm gold + white — conveys trust, faith, and mission without feeling corporate.
  • Use a proper heading hierarchy (H1 → H2 → H3) — currently headings are inconsistently sized and formatted.
  • Replace icon images with SVG icons or a paid icon pack for crisp scaling on all screen sizes.
  • Ensure all body text passes WCAG AA contrast ratio — accessibility matters for credibility and Google ranking.
  • Add micro-animations on scroll for impact counters and section reveals — these increase time on site and perceived professionalism.
  • Mobile: the current mobile nav likely needs a hamburger menu — confirm and fix any tap target size issues for all CTAs.
